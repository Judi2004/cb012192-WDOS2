Sea Turtle Hatchery & Rescue Center

The Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Centre in Hikkaduwa, Sri Lanka is a project dedicated to the conservation and protection of sea turtles. Main task of the sea turtle hatchery and rescue centre are all state-of-the-art, complete with water tanks to take care for newborn turtles, along with adult turtle species that have been rescued from the sea due to injuries.Once the eggs hatch,the baby turtles are placed in a tank until they are ready to be released into the ocean.Also the injured turtles at the hatchery are followed up by veterninarians.

It's important to note that while visiting the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, it is essential to follow the guidelines and instructions provided by the staff to ensure the well-being and conservation of the turtles. Respecting the natural behavior and habitat of the turtles is crucial to their long-term survival.

By combining hatchery operations, turtle rescue efforts, education, and research, the Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Centre in Hikkaduwa plays a significant role in the conservation and preservation of sea turtles in the region.

Things to do in the Hikkaduwa turtle hatchery

  • Tour around the hatchery

    • You get the chance of observing the hatchery and learn about sea turtles,their life cycle and the conservation actions take by the hatchery.

  • Egg incubation and release

    • You may get the opportunity to witness the incubation process,depending on the season.At the hatchery you can also get the chance to observe the collected eggs are incubated in special nests.Sometimes you may witness the release of hatchlings into the ocean.

  • Turtle Feeding

    • You can participate in feeding sessions where you are capable of hand-feed the sea turtles at the hatchery.This is a great chance to interact with the sea turtles in the hatchery.

About us

We are here to share and give the community a full understanding about the sea turtles in the world and mainly focussing on the sri lankan sea turtles who are really in need. Sea turtles have been around for millions of years. Experts predict all of the different species may go extint unless successful sea turtle conservation programs are established and maintained.So lets spread this message and overcome the challenge. Contribute as much as possible to save the sea turtles.
